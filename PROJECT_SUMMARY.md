## 🎯 ANSWERING EXPECTED QUESTIONS

**Q: Why did you choose Naive Bayes over other algorithms?**
**A**: "Naive Bayes is excellent for text classification, works well with small datasets, is computationally efficient, and achieves high accuracy. It's also interpretable, which helps understand what makes reviews fake."

**Q: How would you improve this with more time?**
**A**: "I would expand the dataset to thousands of reviews, try deep learning models like LSTM or BERT, add multilingual support, and implement real-time monitoring capabilities."

**Q: Can sophisticated fake reviews fool your system?**
**A**: "The current system works on clear patterns. Very sophisticated fakes that perfectly mimic genuine reviews could be challenging. That's why continuous training with new data is important, and why deep learning models could help in future versions."

**Q: How does TF-IDF work?**
**A**: "TF-IDF measures word importance by looking at how often a word appears in a document (TF) and how rare it is across all documents (IDF). This helps identify distinctive words that separate fake from genuine reviews."

**Q: Why is your accuracy so high?**
**A**: "The patterns in fake reviews are quite distinct - they use repetitive language, excessive punctuation, and urgent buying calls. The model learned these clear patterns well. In real-world scenarios with more diverse data, accuracy might be 85-95%."
